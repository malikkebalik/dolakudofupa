Robbie Robertson Career

Robbie Robertson Career: - Robertson's work with the Band was crucial in the development of the Americana music genre. As a member of the Band, Robertson was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame and the Canadian Music Hall of Fame, and he was honored into Canada's Walk of Fame both with the Band and on his own. Robertson is credited with authoring "The Weight," "The Night They Drove Old Dixie Down," and "Up on Cripple Creek" with the Band, as well as having solo singles with "Broken Arrow" and "Somewhere Down the Crazy River," among many more.

Robertson is best known as a film soundtrack producer and composer for his work with director Martin Scorsese, which began with the rockumentary film The Last Waltz (1978) and continued with a number of dramatic films, including Raging Bull (1980), The King of Comedy (1983), Casino (1995), The Departed (2006), The Wolf of Wall Street (2013), The Irishman (2019), and Killers of the Flower Moon (2023).
