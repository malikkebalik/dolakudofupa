While Joe and his 'Game of Thrones' star wife, Sophie Turner, kept her pregnancy hush-hush, the couple did announce the birth of their baby girl last month through a representative. According to Digital Spy, the spokesperson for the couple shared that Joe and Sophie were "delighted to announce" the happy news.

When it comes to his music outside the Jonas Brothers, Joe has been quiet. His debut solo album 'Fastlife' released back in 2011. His latest single as a solo artiste arrived in 2018 which was a collaboration with DJ and producer Jonas Blue, titled 'I See Love'. 

Joe's band DNCE in which he is frontman has also been fairly quiet. Their self-titled album released in 2016 and had a re-issue in 2017. Their latest project is the EP 'People to People' which released in 2018. However, fans were ecstatic to hear that the Jonas Brothers made a comeback last year with their album 'Happiness Begins' and have been keeping up to date with new music in 2020 as well.

Meanwhile, Sophie Turner revealed in March that she was "deeply" missing 'Game of Thrones' and would "go back to it in a heartbeat" after the series wrapped up last year. "I miss everything [about it]," she said. "I really, deeply, deeply miss it. "I miss the costumes. I miss the set. I miss the feeling that I got when I walked onto set in my costume, and that was incredibly empowering. I miss the people. I miss everything. I would go back to it in a heartbeat."

But the upcoming spin-offs aren't getting much love either. Earlier this year, she said,  "I don't want to be a part of it. Unless they give me the big bucks, I'm not doing it!"
