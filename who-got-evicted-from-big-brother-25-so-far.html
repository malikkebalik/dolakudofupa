Who Got Evicted From Big Brother 25 So Far?

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Wednesday, July 3, 2024

Ndtmusic.edu.vn

Who Got Evicted From Big Brother 25 So Far: Every week, the tension rises as one houseguest is sent packing during the live episode of ‘Big Brother.’ The evictions can be unpredictable, leaving fans on the edge of their seats. From unanimous votes to shocking twists, the game is full of surprises. So, who has been evicted so far? Let’s dive in and find out!

Big Brother Season 25 Evictions

Week 1 – Kirsten Elwin


The first week of Big Brother Season 25 saw the eviction of Kirsten Elwin. Despite her efforts to rally some of the houseguests to vote in her favor, Kirsten found herself on the receiving end of a unanimous vote. With no allies in the game, she became an easy target for the other contestants. It was a tough break for Kirsten, who didn’t even get a chance to experience life in the jury house.

Week 2 – Reilly Smedley


In Week 2, Reilly Smedley, who had held the Head of Household position, was sent packing. It seemed like a case of karma catching up to her, as she had spent the previous week convincing the houseguests to vote out Kirsten. The other players saw this as an opportunity to strike back and unanimously voted for Reilly’s eviction. It was a reminder that alliances can quickly shift in the game of Big Brother.

Week 3 – Hisam Goueli


Week 3 brought another unanimous eviction in the form of Hisam Goueli. With an 11-0 vote, Hisam was sent home from the Big Brother house. While fans were sad to see him go, Hisam’s reaction to discovering a secret between Cirie and Jared provided some comedic relief. It was a memorable moment in a season filled with unexpected twists and turns.

Week 4 – Jag Bains


Jag Bains faced a unanimous vote for eviction in Week 4. However, due to the Power of Invincibility being used, Jag was able to stay in the house. This led to a unique situation where the entire house, including the outgoing Head of Household, had to compete in the HoH competition. It was a tense week for all the contestants as they fought to secure their positions in the game.

Week 5 – Red Utley


Breaking away from the pattern of unanimous evictions, Red Utley was voted out of the Big Brother house with a vote of 8-2 in Week 5. Red expressed his disappointment during his exit interview, specifically calling out Cam for betraying him. The revelation of alliances and the resulting tension added a new layer of complexity to the game.

Week 6 – Izzy Gleicher


In Week 6, Izzy Gleicher became the latest houseguest to be evicted, with a vote of 8-1. She left the house feeling betrayed by Cory, whom she had considered a close ally. The game of Big Brother is known for its shifting dynamics and the importance of trust, and Izzy’s eviction served as a reminder of the unpredictable nature of the competition.

Week 7 – Cameron Hardin and Jared Fields


Week 7 brought a double eviction, resulting in the eviction of two houseguests. Cameron Hardin was the first to be evicted with a vote of 8-0. The fast-paced nature of double evictions adds an extra layer of excitement to the game, as the remaining contestants must quickly adapt to the changing dynamics. Following Cameron’s eviction, Jared Fields was also voted out by a vote of 6-1. The double eviction week marked a significant turning point in the season.

Week 8 – Jared Fields


After a zombie battleback twist competition, Cameron Hardin fought his way back into the game, leading to Jared Fields’ eviction by default in Week 8. This marked Jared’s second eviction from the Big Brother house. The unexpected twists and turns of the game kept the houseguests on their toes and provided viewers with thrilling moments.

Week 9 – Mecole Hayes


Week 9 saw another unanimous eviction, with Mecole Hayes being voted out by a vote of 7-0. Before her departure, Mecole exposed a major alliance in the game, causing a potential shake-up in the dynamics of the remaining contestants. The revelation added a new layer of intrigue and strategy to the game.

Week 10 – Cameron Hardin


In Week 10, Cameron Hardin faced his final eviction from the Big Brother house. With a vote of 6-0, Cameron’s journey in the game came to an end. During his exit interview, he took responsibility for his mistakes and jokingly asked if he could re-enter the game. However, his time in the competition had come to a definitive close, as he headed to the jury house.

Week 11 – Cory Wurtenberger


In Week 11, Cory Wurtenberger was evicted from the Big Brother house with a vote of 5-0. While his departure may not have come as a surprise to many, it marked another significant moment in the season. The game of Big Brother is known for its unexpected twists and turns, and Cory’s eviction added to the ever-changing dynamics of the competition.

Double Evictions on Big Brother

Explanation of Double Evictions

Double evictions are one of the most exciting and unpredictable events in the game of Big Brother. During a double eviction, not one, but two houseguests are sent home in a single night. The fast-paced nature of double evictions adds a thrilling element to the game, as the remaining contestants must navigate through multiple competitions and decisions in a short amount of time.

After the regularly scheduled eviction takes place, the Head of Household and Veto competitions are played back-to-back. The houseguests must strategize quickly and make split-second decisions to secure their safety or target their opponents. The tension and suspense during a double eviction are palpable, as the stakes are higher and the game can change dramatically in just a matter of minutes.

Anticipation for Double Evictions

Fans of Big Brother eagerly anticipate double evictions each season. The element of surprise and the potential for game-changing moments make double evictions must-watch episodes. Viewers are kept on the edge of their seats, wondering who will be evicted and how the remaining houseguests will react to the sudden shift in dynamics.

The anticipation for double evictions stems from the excitement of seeing the game accelerate and witnessing the houseguests’ strategic moves under intense pressure. It’s a chance for unexpected alliances to form, secret plans to be revealed, and power dynamics to shift dramatically. Double evictions inject a burst of energy into the season and leave fans eagerly awaiting the next twist in the game.

Big Brother Season 25 Schedule

Weekly Airings on CBS

Big Brother Season 25 airs on CBS with a weekly schedule that keeps fans eagerly tuning in for the latest episodes. The show follows a consistent pattern, providing viewers with a regular dose of drama, competition, and strategic gameplay.

On Sundays at 10 p.m. EST, fans can catch the latest developments in the Big Brother house. This episode offers a recap of the week’s events, showcasing the alliances, conflicts, and game-changing moments that have unfolded.

Tuesdays at 8 p.m. EST mark another important airing of Big Brother. This episode delves deeper into the strategic gameplay, showcasing the Head of Household competition, the nomination ceremony, and the Power of Veto competition. Viewers get a front-row seat to the intense battles for power and the strategic moves that shape the game.

Thursdays at 9 p.m. EST are the highlight of the week for Big Brother fans. This is when the live eviction episode takes place, where the fate of the houseguests hangs in the balance. Viewers witness the tension and anticipation as the votes are cast, leading to the eviction of a contestant. The live episode also includes Diary Room commentary, providing insights into the contestants’ thoughts and strategies.

The weekly airings on CBS offer fans a chance to immerse themselves in the captivating world of Big Brother Season 25. With each episode, viewers are treated to a thrilling blend of competition, strategy, and unexpected twists that keep them hooked until the season’s exciting conclusion.
