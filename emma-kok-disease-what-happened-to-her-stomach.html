What Happened To Emma Kok’s Stomach?

Emma Kok was afflicted with gastroparesis in her early years, which caused damage to her stomach. Partial paralysis of the stomach muscles is a medical disorder called gastroparesis. It hinders or slows down the body’s ability to properly break down and process food through the digestive tract.

Emma’s stomach is rendered inert as a result of this condition, which poses significant obstacles for the digestive system. The intricacy of gastroparesis stems from an interruption in the nerves controlling the stomach’s movement.

Normally, food is broken down in the stomach by contracting and mixing it with digestive enzymes for processing. Emma’s body experiences hindered contractions, making it difficult for her stomach to process even small amounts of food. This disease makes eating challenging and has an impact on the amount of nutrients consumed overall since it can produce several symptoms, including bloating, vomiting, nausea, and fullness.

Emma has two feeding pumps to provide the vital nutrition her body requires to function to manage this illness. These pumps transfer the necessary nutrients into her digestive system, circumventing the limited capacity of her stomach.
