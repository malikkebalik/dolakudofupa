How to Buy the Right Argan Oil for Your Face?

If you don't have the proper knowledge, you might end up buying a product not suitable for your skin. Therefore, we bring you a few factors that'll help you pick the best argan oil for your face.

Purity

Argan oil is a natural product, and it is essential to check its purity. The best oils are made using 100% pure and natural materials with no alcohol, additives, or fragrance. These oils are not diluted to keep the purity at its best. Many brands also manufacture paraben-free, sulfate-free, petrolatum-free, and mineral oil-free oils to make them suit even the most sensitive skin types. Furthermore, you can find 100% vegan argan oil if you are vegan.

Uses

Different brands manufacture different types of argan oils to work best on different body parts. Some claim to work wonders on your skin, while others might deliver great results in treating your scalp issues, hair problems, or nails related concerns. You can also find some multi-purpose products that you can use on any part of your body. So, choose a product according to your needs.

Skin suitability

Like any other skincare product, argan oils are manufactured to suit different skin types. Some work best on dry skin, while others are ideal for acne-prone skin. If you have oily skin type or combination skin, you can still find argan oil according to your skin type. So, make sure to read the product's description to find out whether it is meant for your skin or not.

Purpose

Argan oil is a natural product that has anti-sebum effects to regulate the amount of sebum in your skin while protecting it against harmful chemicals, toxins, UV rays, and pollution. It is meant to brighten up your skin and make it glow naturally. Some oils are manufactured using a formula that treats dry skin, inflammation, dark spots, and even fine lines. However, other oils might be suitable to treat acne and other oily-skin-related issues. So, consider your purpose before you buy a particular product.

Environment friendly

If you want to do your bit towards saving the environment, you should buy oil that is sensitive to environmental conservation. Many brands sell cruelty-free products that are not tested on animals, unlike your regular cosmetics. They are also made using sustainable materials, causing no harm to the environment. So, choose eco-friendly natural products to join hands with people working towards this cause.

Guarantee

The top brands fully guarantee to provide a full refund if you are not satisfied with the product or feel it didn't deliver the results as promised. So, make sure to check this aspect to get optimum satisfaction after purchasing it.

What Are the Benefits of Using Argan Oil on Your Face?

Argan oil is made from the argan seeds that grow on argan trees. It contains vital nutrients, protein, and fatty acids that boost your skin health. Below, we have listed some of the most talked-about benefits of using argan oil on the face.

  • Argan oil has antioxidant properties that prevent burns and hyperpigmentation caused due to exposure to the sun. It also shields your skin against other severe issues like skin cancer.
  • Argan oil contains abundant vitamin E that enhances water retention in your skin and keeps it moisturized.
  • It has antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties to treat various inflammatory skin conditions like rosacea and psoriasis.
  • Argan oil is also known for its antibacterial and fungicidal properties that prevent various bacterial and fungal infections. Since ancient times, it has been used to get rid of skin infections.
  • The powerful combination of vitamin E and antioxidants found in argan oil can do wonders in healing wounds.
  • Argan oil contains antioxidant properties that improve skin elasticity, preventing and reducing stretch marks.

How Much Does Argan Oil for Your Face Cost?

The cost of argan oil may vary depending on its purity, purpose, quantity, and guarantee offered by the brand. Some of the best brands sell argan oil within the price range of $9 to $20. The price may also fluctuate based on skin suitability, as some products are suitable for a particular skin type, while others might suit all skin types, including sensitive skin. In this case, the latter would be pricier.
