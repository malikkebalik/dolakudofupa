Zlatan Ibrahimović biography.

Zlatan Ibrahimović is a professional Swedish footballer who plays as a striker for both his National Team and club side, AC Milan.

The Iconic Striker started his professional football journey in 1999 when he joined Malmö FF in the Allsvenskan. He stayed there for 3 years and made a total of 47 appearances with 18 goals to his name for the club.

Zlatan then moved to the Eredivisie in 2001-2002 by joining Ajax. After 4 years with the Eredivisie club, he pulled off 110 appearances and scored a total of 48 goals throughout all competitions for Ajax.

His outstanding performance gained the attention of the Italian Serie A giants, Juventus. In 2004-2005, Juventus signed the amazing goalscorer and he stayed with them for 2 years making a total of 92 appearances with 26 goals to his name before departing the club.

Ibrahimović then joined Inter Milan in 2006-2007 for 3 years in the Italian Serie A. He scored 66 goals in his 117 appearances for Inter Milan in all competitions.

He then made a move to the Spanish La Liga by joining FC Barcelona in 2009-2010. He spent only 2 years there and made 46 appearances with 22 goals to his name. However, during his second season, he was loaned out to AC Milan.

Zlatan first joined AC Milan in the Italian Serie A in 2010-2011 as a loan player and later signed a deal to be a permanent player. He scored 56 goals in his 85 appearances for the club.
