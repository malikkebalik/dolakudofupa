Where was Viggo Mortensen born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

October 20, 1958, saw the birth of Viggo Mortensen in New York City, USA. His full name is Viggo Peter Mortensen Jr., and he is an American citizen.

Based on his astrological chart, he is 65 years old and was born under the sign of Libra. He is of Danish, English, and Scottish origin, and he has dual citizenship with the United States.

His parents are named Viggo Peter Mortensen (father) and Grace Gamble Atkinson (mother). In addition, he was reared in Venezuela and Denmark. After his parents split, he moved to New York with his mother.

He attended the high school in Watertown. Subsequently, he enrolled at St. Lawrence University, where he earned a bachelor’s degree in politics and Spanish studies in 1980.

How did Viggo Mortensen start his Professional Career?

Actor and producer Viggo Mortensen’s professional background includes selling flowers in Copenhagen and driving trucks in Esbjerg. Later, he made his acting debut, although with a few deleted sequences, in the movie “The Purple Rose of Cairo.”

Thus, he portrayed an Amish farmer in Peter Weir’s 1985 neo-noir criminal thriller film “Witness,” which became his debut. He portrayed Bragg in the American television soap opera “Search for Tomorrow” the following year.

In addition, he has established himself in the entertainment industry by playing both significant and supporting parts in movies and TV shows since then. Among his notable films are “The Indian Runner” (1991), “Carlito’s Way” (1993), “Crimson Tide” (1995), “Daylight” (1996), “The Portrait of a Lady” (1996), “G.I. Jane” (1997), “A Perfect Murder” (1998), “A Walk on the Moon” (1999), and “28 Days” (2000).

After starring as Aragorn in the widely watched trilogy of epic fantasy adventure films “The Lord of the Rings” (2001–2003), he became well-known around the world.

His work with director David Cronenberg on the thrillers “A History of Violence” (2005), “Eastern Promises” (2007)—for which he was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actor—and “A Dangerous Method” (2011) made him well-known.

In addition, he received an Academy Award nomination for his leading roles as Tony Lip in “Green Book” (2018) and as an anarchist dad in “Captain Fantastic” (2016). In 2020, he both appeared in and directed his first film, “With Falling.”

In addition to acting, he has experimented with visual arts, poetry, photography, and music. He established the Perceval Press to publish works by lesser-known writers and artists in addition to his own.

He collaborated on many albums with guitarist Buckethead and performed and composed music for the “The Lord of the Rings” soundtrack.
