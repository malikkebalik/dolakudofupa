Early Life

Born Benjamin Hammond Haggerty on June 19, 1983, in Seattle, Washington, Macklemore’s affinity for rap music was ignited at a young age. Drawing inspiration from iconic artists such as Nas, Mobb Deep, and the Wu-Tang Clan, he honed his craft as a lyricist and performer during his formative years. Adopting the moniker “Professor Macklemore,” he began crafting his own mixtapes and collaborating with fellow artists, laying the foundation for his future success in the music industry.

Macklemore Collaboration with Ryan Lewis

In 2009, Macklemore’s musical trajectory shifted dramatically when he joined forces with producer Ryan Lewis, forming a dynamic creative duo that would redefine the hip-hop landscape. Their partnership yielded a string of critically acclaimed albums and chart-topping singles, including the groundbreaking album “The Heist.” Featuring iconic tracks such as “Thrift Shop” and “Can’t Hold Us,” “The Heist” catapulted Macklemore to international stardom, earning him widespread acclaim and accolades.

Macklemore Social Commentary

Throughout his career, Macklemore has distinguished himself as a socially conscious lyricist, fearlessly tackling pressing issues such as gay rights, materialism, and racial inequality.

Macklemore Awards
