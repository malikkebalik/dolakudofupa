All About Brendon Urie | Net Worth, Relationship, Career

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Monday, July 15, 2024

Brendon, Boyd Brendon Urie is an American singer, songwriter, and musician. He is best known as the lead singer of the rock band Panic! at the Disco. He is the sole surviving original member of the band. He sings in a tenor range that spans four octaves. His range extends from D2 to C7. He has nearly 4.2 million Instagram followers.

What is the Net Worth of Brendon Urie? Salary, Earnings

Brendon Urie sang “Open Happiness” for the Coca-Cola Company in 2008. Martin Guitars has named him as a brand ambassador. His musical profession allows him to make a life. As of 2019, his net worth is expected to be $8 million.

Where was Brendon Urie born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Brendon Urie was born on April 12th, 1987. His given name is Brendon Boyd Urie. His father was Boyd Urie, and his mother was Grace Urie. He has about a quarter of Polynesian ancestry from Hawaii through his mother’s side. Brendon was born in St. George, Utah, in the United States. He is a U.S. citizen. His astrological sign is Aries.

His four older siblings are Matt, Mason, Kyla, and Kara. He is the group’s youngest member. His family moved to Las Vegas when he was two years old.

He went to Palo Verde High School in Las Vegas. In high school, he labeled himself a “spaz.”

Is Brendon Urie Single? Relationship

Brendon Urie is a father and a husband. His wife’s name is Sarah Orzechowski. He met Sarah on one of his gigs when she was in a relationship with someone else. Sarah was single when they met later on Urie’s show.

He later revealed that he wrote Sarah Smiles from the album Vices & Virtues specifically to impress her. In September 2011, they announced their engagement. The couple married on April 27, 2013. He described himself as pansexual and acknowledged to having “experimented” with men.

He stated that he was diagnosed with ADHD at a young age and was given medication to address the condition.

How tall is Brendon Urie? Weight, Hair Color

Brendon Urie stands 1.74 m tall, or 5 feet and 8 and a half inches. He is 150 pounds (68 kg). He has an athletic build. Both his eyes and hair are dark brown.

How did Brendon Urie start his Professional Career?

Brent Wilson and Urie met in high school while taking guitar lessons. Wilson approached Urie and encouraged him to audition for the post of Panic! at Disco’s lead guitarist. Ryan Ross was their lead singer. Urie was later chosen as the group’s lead singer.

He began working at Tropical Smoothie Cafe to help fund his band’s rehearsal space. He entertained the customers at the cafe. Panic! at the Disco has produced six studio albums with Urie as the primary vocalist.

The band’s fifth studio album, Death of a Bachelor, was their best-selling week and first number-one record. He wrote “New Perspective” for the soundtrack of the film Jennifer’s Body. Urie appeared as a guest vocalist on The Cab’s “One Of THOSE Nights,” alongside Patrick Stump.

Among the artists for whom he has provided guest vocals are Fall Out Boy, Travie McCoy, Cee-Lo Green, Janelle Monae, Dillon Francis, Lil Dicky, and Every Time I Die (band).

When Billy Joel received the Kennedy Center Honors in December 2013, the nation’s highest honor for influencing American society via the arts, he performed “Big Shot” in front of Billy Joel, President Barack Obama, and an audience.

He has appeared in music videos like Fall Out Boy’s “A Little Less Sixteen Candles, a Little More Touch Me,” “What A Catch, Donnie,” and “Headfirst Slide into Cooperstown on a Bad Bet,” Gym Class Heroes’ “Clothes Off!!,” and Butch Walker’s “Pretty Melody.”

In 2017, he played Charlie Price in Cyndi Lauper’s Tony Award-winning Broadway musical Kinky Boots. He created the song “Not A Simple Sponge” for the SpongeBob SquarePants musical in 2015.
